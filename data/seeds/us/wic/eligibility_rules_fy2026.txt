WIC ELIGIBILITY RULES — POLICY SEED
Source: 7 CFR Part 246 (WIC Program Regulations), USDA FNS
For use in rationale trace generation. Not a complete regulatory text.

=== PROGRAM OVERVIEW ===

WIC (Special Supplemental Nutrition Program for Women, Infants, and Children)
provides nutritious foods, nutrition education, breastfeeding support, and
healthcare referrals to income-eligible participants.

=== PARTICIPANT CATEGORIES (7 CFR 246.7(a)) ===

Eligible categories:
  1. Pregnant women
  2. Breastfeeding women (up to infant's 1st birthday)
  3. Postpartum women (up to 6 months after delivery, not breastfeeding)
  4. Infants (up to 1 year of age)
  5. Children (age 1 through 4 years, 11 months = "under age 5")

Ineligible categories: Any person not in the above categories.

=== INCOME ELIGIBILITY (7 CFR 246.7(d)) ===

INCOME LIMIT: 185% of Federal Poverty Guidelines

FY2026 INCOME LIMITS (effective Oct 1, 2025):
  Household Size | Monthly Gross Income Limit
  1              | $2,322/month
  2              | $3,140/month
  3              | $3,959/month
  4              | $4,777/month
  5              | $5,595/month
  6              | $6,414/month
  7              | $7,232/month
  8              | $8,051/month
  Each add'l     | +$819/month

NO ASSET TEST: WIC has no resource/asset test.
Income is measured as gross income before deductions.

CATEGORICAL ELIGIBILITY (7 CFR 246.7(d)(2)):
  A person is automatically income-eligible if they or any member of their
  immediate family participates in:
    - SNAP (Food Stamps)
    - Medicaid
    - TANF (Temporary Assistance for Needy Families)
  Note: Categorical eligibility satisfies the INCOME test only.
  Nutritional risk determination is STILL required.

=== NUTRITIONAL RISK (7 CFR 246.7(e)) ===

REQUIRED FOR ALL APPLICANTS — income eligibility alone is insufficient.
A health professional at the WIC clinic must determine nutritional risk.

Nutritional risk conditions include (non-exhaustive):
  - Abnormal nutritional conditions (anemia, underweight, overweight)
  - Dietary deficiencies
  - Medical conditions affecting nutritional needs
  - Homeless, migrant worker status

=== RESIDENCY (7 CFR 246.7(g)) ===

Applicant must be a resident of the state in which they are applying.
No minimum residency duration required. Intent to reside is sufficient.

=== BENEFITS ===

WIC provides food packages (not cash). Food packages vary by category:
  - Pregnant/postpartum: milk, eggs, cereal, juice, beans/peanut butter, vegetables/fruits
  - Breastfeeding: enhanced package with fish, more dairy
  - Infants: iron-fortified formula (if not breastfeeding), infant cereal, fruits/vegetables
  - Children: milk, eggs, cereal, juice, beans/peanut butter, vegetables/fruits

WIC also provides: nutrition education, breastfeeding support, healthcare referrals.
