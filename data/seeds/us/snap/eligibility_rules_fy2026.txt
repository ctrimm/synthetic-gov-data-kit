=== INCOME ELIGIBILITY (7 CFR 273.9) ===

GROSS INCOME TEST (7 CFR 273.9(a)(1)):
  A household's monthly gross income must not exceed 130 percent of the
  Federal poverty guidelines. Households that contain an elderly person
  (age 60 or older) or a person who receives certain disability payments
  are EXEMPT from the gross income test.

NET INCOME TEST (7 CFR 273.9(a)(2)):
  A household's net monthly income must not exceed 100 percent of the
  Federal poverty guidelines. This applies to ALL households including
  those with elderly or disabled members.

=== DEDUCTIONS (7 CFR 273.9(c)) ===

(c)(1) EARNED INCOME DEDUCTION: 20 percent of earned income is deducted.
  Earned income = wages, salaries, self-employment net earnings.

(c)(2) STANDARD DEDUCTION (FY2026, 48 States + DC):
  Household sizes 1-3: $209/month
  Household size 4:    $223/month
  Household size 5:    $261/month
  Household size 6+:   $299/month

(c)(3) DEPENDENT CARE DEDUCTION: Actual dependent care costs paid for
  a child or other dependent when necessary for household member to work,
  seek work, or attend training/education. No cap.

(c)(4) MEDICAL EXPENSE DEDUCTION: For elderly/disabled household members,
  medical expenses exceeding $35/month may be deducted. No cap.
  Medical expenses include: doctor visits, prescriptions, Medicare premiums,
  dentures, hearing aids, prosthetics, transportation to medical care.

(c)(5) EXCESS SHELTER DEDUCTION:
  Shelter costs exceeding 50% of the household's income after other deductions
  may be deducted, up to a cap of $744/month (FY2026, 48 States + DC).
  Cap does NOT apply to households with elderly or disabled members.
  Shelter costs include: rent, mortgage, taxes, insurance, utilities.

=== ASSETS / RESOURCES (7 CFR 273.8) ===

ASSET LIMITS (FY2026, UNCHANGED from FY2025):
  General households:         $3,000
  Elderly/disabled households: $4,500
  
  Countable assets include: cash, bank accounts, stocks, bonds.
  Excluded assets include: home, vehicle (one), retirement accounts,
  SNAP benefits, most IRA/pension funds.

BROAD-BASED CATEGORICAL ELIGIBILITY (BBCE):
  Many states have adopted BBCE, which removes or raises the asset test.
  In BBCE states, households receiving (or deemed to receive) TANF-funded
  services are categorically eligible. Most BBCE states remove the asset
  test entirely for most households.

=== CATEGORICAL ELIGIBILITY ===

Households in which ALL members receive SSI, TANF, or certain GA programs
are categorically eligible for SNAP without separate income/asset testing.
BBCE extends this to households receiving TANF-funded services.

=== BENEFIT CALCULATION ===

Monthly benefit = Maximum allotment − (Net income × 0.30)
  (Households are expected to spend 30% of net income on food.)

Minimum benefit for 1-2 person households: $24/month (FY2026).

=== WORK REQUIREMENTS ===

Able-Bodied Adults Without Dependents (ABAWDs):
  Must work or participate in work activities ≥ 80 hours/month, or be
  exempt. The One Big Beautiful Bill Act of 2025 modified work requirement
  rules — consult current FNS guidance for latest provisions.
